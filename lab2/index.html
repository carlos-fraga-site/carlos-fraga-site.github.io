How to Replace a Single-Pole Light Switch!

By: Carlos Fraga


Step 1: Locate Breaker Box

The first step is to locate the breaker box, most of the time it will be located in the garage.

warningOnce that is located, turn off the breaker to be safe. Return to the wall switch to confirm that the power has been turned off.

Step 2: Remove Plastic Cover Plate
The second step is to use a flathead screw driver to remove the 2 screws which are holding the plastic cover plate in place.
Step 3: Remove 2 Screws on Light Switch
The third step consists of utilizing a phillips-head screwdriver to remove the two screws on the light swtich so that you can extract the switch to access the copper wires/connectors.
Step 4: Wrap Copper Wires
The fourth step is to bend the copper wires in a u-shape so that they wrap around the screws, then tighten the screws until the copper wire is making contact with the connectors.
Step 5: Cover Switch with Plastic Cover
The fifth and final step is to screw the switch back into the junction box, and lastly screw the plastic cover plate over the switch. Restore power and check if you have light, if so success, if not you will need to troubleshoot.
